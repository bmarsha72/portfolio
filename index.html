Plays Well With Others

Coders have a reputation for militant devotion to libraries, languages, sci-fi charachter, and/or coffee brands. Due to my late arrival to the field, cheery disposition, and proper training, I remain entirely agnostic in my technology leanings. From the javascript/html love-child syntax of React to the underground MongoDB geospacial abilities nobody seems to know about, I'm willing to learn anything. I'll even learn PHP. And I get along great with people, too.

Fully Stacked

"Full stack" is a term that gets a lot of airplay these days. In my case, it means I am able to create, from a blank piece of paper, a fully robust website, from my own wireframe, ERD, relational database, and MVC architecture. Displayed, for example, using sweet parallax views like the one you're looking at right now (I think I decided to travel to Patagonia and Iceland because I subconsciously knew I'd need my own free stock photos like these some day).

Immersion Anodized

Coding immersion programs have been around for a few years, no doubt. But they vary widely in their virility. What sold me on the Web Development Immersive program at General Assembly was the breadth and depth of their program, their global presence, the caliber of their staff, their outcomes program, backing by Adam Pritzker, GSV Capital, Institutional Investors, Maveron, and the fact that they don't inflate thier new graduate salary figures by locating themselves in San Francisco.
