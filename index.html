The Crucible

Coding immersion programs have been around for a few years, no doubt. But they vary widely in their virility. What sold me on the Web Development Immersive program at General Assembly was the breadth and depth of their program, their selectivity (they accept a third of their applicants), their global presence, the caliber of their staff, their outcomes program, and backing by Adam Pritzker, GSV Capital, Institutional Investors, Maveron, and the fact that they don't inflate thier new graduate salary figures by locating themselves in San Francisco.
